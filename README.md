# 6-Month Body Transformation Plan

**Subject:** 32M, 175 cm, 87 kg, BMI 28.4, Hyderabad (Kondapur), software engineer (9 yrs, sedentary), omnivore, married.
**Window:** 26 weeks (Week 0 prep + Weeks 1–26 execution).
**Goal:** Body composition, not scale weight. Target: visible upper abs, waist < 85 cm, weight ~73–76 kg, lifts at bodyweight bench / 1.3x squat / 1.5x deadlift / 0.6x OHP / 5+ pull-ups, bloodwork improved.

---

## 0. Operating Principles

1. **Adherence > optimization.** A 75%-perfect plan you do for 26 weeks beats a 100%-perfect plan you abandon in week 7.
2. **Identity, not willpower.** "I am a person who lifts and eats deliberately" — not "I am trying to lose weight."
3. **Environment > willpower.** Engineer the surroundings so the right choice is the path of least resistance.
4. **Never miss two in a row.** One missed gym day, one over-eat day, one bad sleep — fine. The day after a miss is the most important day of the week.
5. **The 30-day rule.** Do not change the plan based on data from less than 4 weeks. Bad week = noise. Two bad weeks = signal.
6. **Audit before you adjust.** When stalled, audit adherence first. 90% of stalls are eating-more-than-you-think, not metabolism.
7. **No food is forbidden, only quantities are.** Saturday craving meal exists by design.
8. **Maintenance > deficit during disruption.** Travel, sickness, hometown visits → maintenance mode, never abandon mode.

## 3. Training Program

### Phase 1 — Weeks 1–4: Full-body 3x/week (M/W/F)

**Goal:** Movement quality, tissue prep, neural patterning. **Not** intensity.

**Rules:**
- Every working set capped at **RPE 6–7** (3–4 reps left in tank). You will feel like you're not pushing. That's correct.
- **No barbell deadlift in Weeks 1–2.** Use goblet squats, dumbbell RDLs, hip thrusts, kettlebell swings instead. Add barbell deadlift Week 3.
- **Film every working set** (squat, deadlift, bench, OHP) from side angle. Watch immediately. Compare against reference videos.
- **Mandatory paid form-check session in Week 1** (after first session) and **Week 4** (before phase 2). ₹1,000–2,000 each, single sessions, not packages. Or one session with a sports physio.

**Reference videos** (watch before Week 1):
- Squat — Squat University "How to Squat" + Alan Thrall "How to Squat"
- Deadlift — Alan Thrall "How to Deadlift" + Squat University "Deadlift Setup"
- Bench — Jeff Nippard "How to Bench Press"
- Overhead Press — Alan Thrall "How to Overhead Press"
- Barbell Row — Jeff Nippard "How to Row"

**Session A (Mon, Wed, Fri — alternate variants A1/A2):**

A1:
1. Goblet squat — 3 × 8–10
2. Dumbbell bench press — 3 × 8–10
3. Lat pulldown or assisted pull-up — 3 × 8–10
4. Dumbbell Romanian deadlift — 3 × 8–10
5. Dumbbell overhead press — 2 × 10
6. Plank — 3 × 30 sec
7. Farmer's carry — 2 × 30 sec

A2:
1. Barbell back squat (empty bar Week 1, +5 kg/week) — 3 × 5
2. Barbell bench press (empty bar Week 1, +2.5 kg/week) — 3 × 5
3. Barbell row — 3 × 8
4. Hip thrust (dumbbell or barbell) — 3 × 10
5. Lat pulldown — 3 × 10
6. Dead bug — 3 × 10/side
7. 5 min easy bike or treadmill walk cooldown

**Week 1**: A1 / A1 / A2.
**Week 2**: A1 / A2 / A1.
**Week 3**: A2 / A1 / A2 — add conventional barbell deadlift (3×5, light) as last lift on one A2 day, replacing hip thrust.
**Week 4**: A2 / A2 / A2 — full barbell template, still RPE 6–7.

**Cardio:**
- 8k → 10k steps/day target ramp (Week 1: 5k, Week 2: 7k, Week 3: 8.5k, Week 4: 10k).
- 2× LISS sessions/week, 30–40 min, on rest days (Tue/Thu or Sun). Treadmill incline walk (3–5° incline, 5–6 km/h), stationary bike (easy pace), or evening walk at park.
- **Never schedule LISS within 6 hours before or 24 hours after a leg-heavy session.**

### Phase 2 — Weeks 5–26: Upper/Lower 4x/week (M/T/Th/F)

**Goal:** Hypertrophy + fat loss. Each muscle group hit 2x/week.

**Progression rule (double progression):**
- Stay at current load until you hit the top of the rep range for **all sets** with good form at RPE 7–8.
- Then add weight: 2.5 kg on upper-body lifts, 5 kg on lower-body lifts.
- Reset reps to bottom of range. Repeat.
- Log every set.

**Day 1 — Upper (push focus):**
1. Barbell bench press — 3 × 5–8
2. Barbell overhead press — 3 × 6–10
3. Incline dumbbell press — 3 × 8–12
4. Dumbbell lateral raise — 3 × 12–15
5. Triceps rope pushdown — 3 × 10–15
6. Cable face pull — 3 × 12–15

**Day 2 — Lower (squat focus):**
1. Barbell back squat — 3 × 5–8
2. Romanian deadlift — 3 × 8–10
3. Walking lunges — 3 × 10/leg
4. Leg curl machine — 3 × 10–12
5. Standing calf raise — 4 × 10–15
6. Hanging knee raise — 3 × 10–15

**Day 3 — Upper (pull focus):**
1. Pull-up (or lat pulldown) — 3 × 5–10
2. Barbell row — 3 × 6–10
3. Seated cable row — 3 × 8–12
4. Dumbbell rear delt fly — 3 × 12–15
5. Barbell or dumbbell curl — 3 × 8–12
6. Hammer curl — 2 × 10–12

**Day 4 — Lower (deadlift focus):**
1. Conventional deadlift — 3 × 3–5 (heavy, with straps if thumb flares)
2. Front squat or goblet squat — 3 × 8–10
3. Hip thrust — 3 × 8–12
4. Bulgarian split squat — 3 × 10/leg
5. Seated calf raise — 4 × 10–15
6. Plank — 3 × 45 sec

**Cardio (Phase 2):**
- 10k steps/day, every day.
- 1× LISS (40 min) — Wednesday or Sunday.
- **Week 9 onward: add 1× HIIT (15–20 min)** — Sunday morning or Wednesday. Options: assault bike intervals (30 sec hard / 90 sec easy × 8), incline treadmill sprints, stair climbs. **Skip if leg day was the previous evening or is the next morning.**

### Strength targets at Week 26 (success criteria):
- Bench press: ≥ bodyweight (~73–76 kg) for 5 reps
- Squat: ≥ 1.3× bodyweight (~95–100 kg) for 5 reps
- Deadlift: ≥ 1.5× bodyweight (~110–115 kg) for 3–5 reps
- Overhead press: ≥ 0.6× bodyweight (~45 kg) for 5 reps
- Pull-up: ≥ 5 clean bodyweight reps

### Injury / pain protocol:
- **Sharp pain = stop the set, end the lift, ice if acute.** Soreness is fine, sharp pain is not.
- Right thumb flare-up: immediately switch to lifting straps for all pulling. Reduce grip-intensive accessories (no farmer's carries, no heavy DB holds) for 1 week.
- Lower back tweak (deadlift): pause deadlifts for 1 week, replace with hip thrust + RDL light. McKenzie press-ups daily. If still painful at 1 week, see physio.
- Knee pain (squat): check depth + knee tracking on video. Switch to high-bar / goblet, drop to bodyweight if needed. If sharp/persistent, see physio.

## 4. Nutrition

### Targets (recalibrated for sedentary baseline)
- **Maintenance:** ~2,300 kcal/day
- **Cut intake:** **1,800 kcal/day** (≈600 kcal deficit, ~600 g fat/week target loss)
- **Protein:** **150 g/day** (target band 140–160 g) — non-negotiable floor
- **Fat:** ~55–65 g/day (~30% of intake)
- **Carbs:** ~180–210 g/day (the remainder)
- **Fiber:** 30 g+/day
- **Water:** 3–4 L/day
- **Salt:** don't restrict — Indian food is salty and you're sweating in the gym; restrict only if BP is elevated on bloodwork

### Phase 1 (Weeks 1–8) — Track everything
- Log every food item in MacroFactor (preferred) or HealthifyMe Pro.
- **Weigh raw weight where possible** (rice, dal, chicken). Cooked weights vary by water content.
- Log oil/ghee explicitly. This is where Indian diets leak 300+ hidden kcal.
- Hit calorie target **± 100 kcal** and protein target **± 10 g** daily.
- Saturday: log the weekday meals. Saturday dinner = unlogged craving meal. Eat without guilt, single meal, stop when full.

### Phase 2 (Weeks 9–26) — Tiffin + plate method
- Switch primary lunches/dinners to a high-protein tiffin service (Hyderabad options: **FreshMenu Fit, Cubes, EatFit, or a custom dabba**). Specify: 40g+ protein per meal, low oil, brown rice or millets where possible.
- Log only when uncertain (eating out, new dish, restaurant). Otherwise eyeball using the calibration from Phase 1.
- **Saturday dinner: now logged, treat as refeed day** at maintenance (~2,300 kcal, 200g+ carbs, still 150g protein). The biryani/pizza fits — log it, eat the rest of the day around it.

### Sample Phase 1 weekday template (~1,800 kcal, ~150g protein)

**Breakfast (~450 kcal, 40g protein):**
- 4 whole eggs + 2 egg whites scrambled in 1 tsp oil
- 1 slice multigrain toast OR 40g oats with water + 1 scoop whey + 10 almonds
- Black coffee or chai (no sugar)

**Lunch (~500 kcal, 45g protein):**
- 150g grilled chicken / fish / paneer (~35g protein)
- 1 cup dal (~8g protein)
- 1 cup mixed sabzi (low oil, 1 tsp)
- 1 small bowl rice (60g raw) OR 2 phulkas (no ghee)
- Salad with lemon

**3:45 PM snack (~180 kcal, 20g protein):** rotate
- 50g roasted chana + black coffee
- 150g Greek yogurt + 30g makhana
- 2 boiled eggs + cucumber + chaat masala
- 1 scoop whey + 1 fruit
- Sprouts chaat (1 cup) with onion/lemon

**Dinner (~500 kcal, 40g protein):**
- 150g chicken / fish / paneer / 200g tofu
- Large vegetable serving (2 cups, low oil)
- 1 phulka OR 50g rice OR skip carbs (your call based on day's remaining macros)
- Curd 100g

**Daily total:** ~1,630 kcal, ~145g protein.
**Buffer:** 170 kcal for chai, fruits, ghee, "small things." Use it deliberately, not by accident.

### Vegetarian-day template (when wife cooks veg, or by choice)

Hitting 150g protein on veg is harder. Protein anchors:
- Paneer (200g = 36g protein)
- Soya chunks (50g dry = 26g protein)
- Tofu (200g = 16–20g protein)
- Dal (1.5 cup cooked = ~14g protein)
- Greek yogurt (150g = 15g protein)
- Whey (2 scoops = 50g protein)
- Eggs if eggetarian (3 eggs = 18g protein)

**Requires deliberate planning** — the default "dal-rice-sabzi" plate has 25–30g protein. You need to add 50–60g more from concentrated sources.

### Hidden-calorie killers (Indian-specific) — track these explicitly during Phase 1
- **Chai with sugar:** 80 kcal × 4 = 320 kcal/day. Switch to no-sugar / stevia, or 1 sugar chai/day cap.
- **Ghee on roti or in dal:** 45 kcal/tsp. Easily 200+ kcal/day invisible.
- **Coconut oil curries, fried tadka, peanuts in poha:** real food, log honestly.
- **"Light" snacks people forget:** dhokla 200 kcal/piece, khakhra 100 kcal each, murmura with sev 300 kcal/cup, 2 Parle-G 140 kcal.
- **Restaurant biryani:** 1,000–1,500 kcal/plate. Saturday only.

### Hydration
- Glass of water on waking.
- Glass before each meal (also reduces appetite).
- 1 L bottle at desk, refilled twice.
- Pre/intra/post workout: ~750 ml.
- Pee should be pale yellow by mid-day. Dark yellow = drink more.

### Constipation rule
- If bowel movements drop on the high-protein diet: 1 tbsp psyllium husk (isabgol) in warm water at night + 2 cups vegetables/day floor + ensure 3 L water.

## 6. Sleep & Recovery

### Hard rules
- **Lights out 12:30 AM weekdays. Wake 7:30–8:00 AM.** ~7–7.5 hours.
- **Weekend drift cap: 1 hour.** Sleep by 1:30 AM, wake by 9:00 AM. Anything beyond this wrecks Monday cortisol and Sunday-night sleep onset.
- **Last caffeine at 1:30 PM.** Lifting-day exception: 1 small black coffee at 6:55 PM (~100 mg). Evening tea-stall hangouts: switch to lemon tea, sulaimani, club soda + lime, or carry decaf bags.
- **Last meal ≥ 2 hours before bed** (10:30 PM hard cap, ideally 9:30 PM). Late dinners worsen snoring and reflux.
- **Bedroom:** dark, cool (24–26°C with AC/fan), no screens 30 min before bed.
- **Phone leaves the bedroom at midnight** OR goes into a drawer in night/grayscale mode. Buy a real alarm clock if needed (₹500).

### Apple Watch Series 11 — sleep apnea screening (replaces oximeter / sleep study at this stage)
- Wear watch to bed every night.
- **At Week 4:** Open Health app → Browse → Respiratory → Breathing Disturbances. Check overnight SpO2 trend.
- **Escalate to a sleep study (polysomnography or Level-3 home test, Apollo / Yashoda / KIMS) immediately if any of:**
  - Apple's "Sleep Apnea Notification" triggers ("elevated breathing disturbances")
  - Overnight SpO2 frequently dips below 90%
  - Wife reports actual breathing **pauses** (not just snoring) — ask her to specifically watch for this for one week
  - You experience daytime sleepiness, morning headache, or feel unrefreshed despite 7+ hours

### Stress / cortisol management
- **Sunlight exposure:** 10 min within an hour of waking. Anchors circadian rhythm. More effective than melatonin pills.
- **Walking after meals:** 10–15 min after lunch and dinner. Improves glucose response and digestion.
- **No work email after 11 PM.** Wind-down requires the brain to disengage.
- **One screen-free wind-down activity:** book / journaling / light stretch / conversation with wife. Pick one, install as a ritual.

### Stall protocol (≥ 2 weeks no movement on weight AND waist AND lifts)
1. **Adherence audit first** (90% of cases):
   - Were Mon–Fri actually clean? Honest count of "small" off-plan items.
   - Were portions weighed during Phase 1, or eyeballed?
   - Was the 4 PM snack pre-emptive, or did samosas slip in?
   - Did Saturday craving meal stay one meal, or expand into Sunday?
   - Are steps actually 10k, or 6k with optimistic memory?
2. **If audit shows leakage** → fix the leak. Do not adjust calories.
3. **If audit shows true adherence** → drop daily calories by **100 only** (or add 1 LISS session). Reassess in 2 weeks.
4. **Never drop calories by more than 100 at a time.** Aggressive cuts crash leptin and adherence.
5. If 4 weeks of true-adherence stall → check thyroid (free T3/T4, TSH) early, don't wait for Month 6.

### Failure trigger (Month 3 review)
At end of Week 12, if **all three** are true:
- Lost < 3 kg from baseline AND
- Waist dropped < 3 cm AND
- No lift has progressed > 20% from baseline

→ **Structural review.** Re-audit adherence first. If adherence is true: pull bloodwork forward, consider sleep study if not yet done, evaluate program (very rarely the issue).

### Success definition (Week 26)
- Weight: 73–76 kg
- Waist: < 85 cm
- Visible upper abs (top 2 visible, lower abs emerging)
- Bench ≥ bodyweight × 5 reps; Squat ≥ 1.3× bodyweight × 5; Deadlift ≥ 1.5× bodyweight × 3–5; OHP ≥ 0.6× bodyweight × 5; Pull-up ≥ 5 clean reps
- Bloodwork: HbA1c steady or down, lipids improved, fasting insulin lower, D3 in range, T stable or up

## 8. Disruption Protocol — Travel, Hometown, Sickness

### Default mode during disruption: **Maintenance, not deficit, not abandonment.**

### Vacation (~10 days, planned)
- Calories: maintenance ~2,300 kcal.
- Protein floor: 120 g/day.
- Movement floor: 30 min walking daily + 2–3 hotel-gym sessions if available (full-body dumbbell, 30 min each). No gym = bodyweight circuit (push-ups, squats, lunges, plank) 3× during the trip.
- **Drinking cap: 2 days max.** Clear spirits + soda over beer/cocktails. Never two consecutive drinking days.
- **Re-entry rule:** day after vacation = normal plan. **No "compensation cut."**

### Hometown visits (~7 days cumulative across the 6 months)
- Tell parents/in-laws ahead: "I'm eating differently for 6 months. Please don't take it personally if I skip seconds."
- Yes to home veggies, dal, rice/roti. No (or one helping only) to fried items, sweets, multiple servings.
- "I already ate" is a complete sentence.
- 15-min walk after every meal. Break the post-meal sitting pattern.
- Keep visits ≤ 2 nights when possible. Longer = treat as full maintenance mode.

### Sickness (cold, fever, food poisoning)
- Drop to maintenance calories (not deficit).
- Skip lifting until 24 hours symptom-free.
- Walk if energy permits, otherwise rest.
- Hydrate aggressively.

### Diwali (~1 week, calendar-aware)
- Pre-planned **maintenance week.** Do not try to cut through Diwali — you'll lose.
- Eat slowly, one helping of sweets per gathering, decline second servings.
- Walk 10k+ steps daily.
- Resume deficit the week after.

### Diet breaks (built-in, not optional)
- **Week 13:** 7-day maintenance break (~2,300 kcal, still 150g protein, lifting continues normally).
- **Week 21:** 7-day maintenance break.
- Scale will rise 1–1.5 kg from glycogen and water during break. **Do not panic. This is hormonal restoration, not fat gain.** Resume deficit Week 14 / Week 22.

## 9. Mental Architecture & Failure Defenses

### Identity, not willpower
- Replace **"I can't eat that"** with **"I don't eat that on weekdays."**
- Replace **"I'm trying to lose weight"** with **"I am a person who lifts and eats deliberately."**

### Public commitment, controlled scope
- Tell wife, gym friend, possibly one work colleague who lifts. **Not Instagram.**
- The wife specifically: she sees real-time eating.
- No public broadcast — premature praise satisfies dopamine without the work.

### Streak tracking + recovery rule
- Daily binary tracker: kcal hit, protein hit, workout/steps done.
- **Never miss two in a row.** The day after a miss is the most important day of the week.

### Rest day is "active recovery," not "off"
- Rest day still has: 10k steps, 150g protein, calorie target, 7+ hours sleep. Only thing absent: gym session.

### Pre-committed responses (implementation intentions)

| Scenario | Response |
|---|---|
| Team lunch at restaurant | Order grilled chicken / paneer tikka + dal + 1 roti + salad. Skip rice. Glass of water before food arrives. Order before others (no menu drift). |
| 4 PM craving | Eat the planned 3:45 snack (already on schedule). |
| Wife/family makes pakoras/biryani on a weekday | One small portion, logged, no seconds. Or "save me one for Saturday." |
| Friend insists on outside chai at 8 PM | Lemon tea / sulaimani / black coffee (only on lifting nights, capped at 100 mg) / club soda + lime. |
| Got home late, missed 7 PM gym | Don't try to lift at 9:30 PM. Reschedule to next morning 6:30 AM. Eat dinner, sleep on time. |
| Office snack drawer has biscuits | Don't open drawer. Replace contents with roasted chana. |
| Travel for work | Hotel breakfast: eggs + protein. One walk daily. Hotel gym 30 min if available. Maintenance, not progress. |
| Wedding / function | Eat protein meal before going. Go directly to non-fried items. One small dessert plate. Don't track. Resume next morning. |
| Sick | Maintenance calories. Skip lifting. Walk if energy. Resume when 24h symptom-free. |
| Cheat day spiraled | Next morning: normal breakfast, 10k steps, normal training. **Do not "compensate"** by under-eating next day — triggers binge cycle. |
| Stuck in a long meeting that ran past 7:30 PM | Notify mentally: "tomorrow morning lift." Eat dinner on schedule. Sleep on schedule. Lift 6:30 AM next day. |

### Milestone rewards (non-food)
- **Month 1:** New flat-soled gym shoes (if not bought in Week 0) OR new lifting belt (Week 8+).
- **Month 3:** A fitted shirt in your new size.
- **Month 6:** A weekend trip OR new piece of equipment OR new watch band — your call. **Never food.**

### Weekly review ritual (Sunday, 15 min)
- Check 7-day weight average vs. previous week.
- Check tape (biweekly).
- Check workout log: did all lifts progress?
- Note one thing that worked, one thing that didn't.
- Plan next week's meals (rough), gym schedule, and any social events to pre-handle.

Frame this like a sprint retrospective. You're an engineer. Use the muscle memory.

### The 30-day rule
- **Do not change the plan based on data from less than 4 weeks.** A bad week is noise. Two bad weeks may be signal. People who tweak constantly never accumulate the consistency that produces results.

## 10. Wife Conversation Script (Week 0 deliverable)

Run this as a calm 15-minute conversation, not an announcement. Adjust language to your style — content matters, not phrasing.

> "I want to do something serious for the next 6 months — work on my fitness and body. I've put together a plan with a lot of structure, and I want to share it with you because it'll affect how I eat and what we do.
>
> I'm aiming to lose body fat and build some muscle. Not a crash diet — a sustainable plan with a Saturday meal where I happily eat anything we want, including the things I'm skipping during the week. That Saturday dinner is going to be **our** meal — you pick the place or the dish.
>
> A few things I'd appreciate, when it's easy:
> - When you make shared dishes, slightly less oil/ghee where it doesn't ruin the taste.
> - I'll prep my own protein side (chicken/paneer/eggs) so you don't have to cook two different things.
> - I might weigh my food during the first 8 weeks. It looks weird but it's temporary — I just need to learn portions.
>
> When I say no to something during the week, it's not me rejecting your cooking. It's the plan. The food gets saved for Saturday or skipped. That's all.
>
> If you want to join me in any part of this — even just walks, even just on weekends — I'd love that. Zero pressure either way. I'll do this regardless, but it's better if you know what's happening."

If she pushes back on specific items: hold the structure (Saturday is shared, weekday is plan), negotiate on details (cooking style, what you eat together).

## 12. Anti-Patterns (do not do these)

- **Crash diets, < 1,500 kcal/day.** Crashes leptin, T, lifts, and adherence. Slower is faster.
- **Two consecutive cheat days.** "Saturday + Sunday" is the most common diet killer.
- **Skipping protein on busy days.** Protein floor is non-negotiable; everything else is flexible.
- **Switching programs every 2 weeks.** You won't see results from any of them. The 30-day rule.
- **"Spot reduction" — abs work to lose belly fat.** Doesn't exist. Calorie deficit + compounds reveal abs.
- **Lifting in running shoes.** Compromises squat and deadlift mechanics.
- **Training through sharp pain.** Soreness is fine; sharp/joint pain means stop.
- **Pre-workout powders / fat burners / "T boosters" / SARMs / steroids.** All rejected.
- **Trusting a friend's "looks fine" over filmed working sets.** You film. You watch. You correct.
- **Comparing scale day-to-day.** Use the 7-day rolling average.
- **Posting progress to Instagram in week 2.** Premature dopamine, satisfies the loop without the work.
- **Lying to the food log.** Lying to others becomes lying to yourself, then the deficit isn't real.
- **Skipping the weekly review.** Without iteration, you're trying — not executing.
- **Dropping calories aggressively after a stall.** -100 max, 2-week reassess.
- **Compensation cuts after a cheat day.** Triggers binge cycle.
- **"I'll start Monday" indefinitely.** Plan starts after Week 0 medical clearance, not after the next event.
